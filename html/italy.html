A Few Pins Along Our Well Traveled Path:

  • Rome
  • Milan
  • Venice
  • Pompeii

Spending Christmas in Italy was like walking through a living postcard, each city wrapped in its own kind of wonder. We began in Pisa, where the Leaning Tower stood proudly in the Piazza dei Miracoli, and the surrounding architecture felt timeless under soft winter skies. From there, we made our way to Rome just in time for the holiday magic to truly begin. On Christmas Day, we stood among thousands in St. Peter's Square in Vatican City, which shimmered with reverence and quiet anticipation, to receive the Papal Blessing‚Äîa truly unforgettable moment.

Over the course of the week, we explored ancient ruins, wandered the halls of history, and tasted the warmth of Italian hospitality. From tossing a coin into the Trevi Fountain to standing beneath the colossal arches of the Colosseum, Rome offered one marvel after another. A day trip to Pompeii brought haunting beauty as we walked the stone streets of a city frozen in time. Later, the scenic drive along the Sorrentine coastline revealed breathtaking views over the Bay of Naples. We ended the journey in Florence, where Renaissance masterpieces, quiet piazzas, and the resting places of Michelangelo and Galileo gave the trip a fitting sense of reflection. It was a holiday unlike any other‚Äîfull of history, spirituality, and a kind of beauty that lingers long after the decorations come down.

The year before, in 2017, we experienced a quieter kind of magic in Venice. Visiting in the off-season meant we saw the floating city at its most tranquil, wrapped in winter's hush. Upon arrival, we were instantly captivated by the network of canals and bridges that replaced cars and crosswalks. Gondolas glided silently past palazzi worn beautifully with age. We wandered through St. Mark's Square, admired the golden mosaics of the Basilica, and watched the sky blush pink over the Grand Canal at dusk. With fewer crowds and a reflective stillness in the air, Venice in winter felt like a dream‚Äîone painted in water, stone, and light.

Our Italian adventures began in 2016 with a short but meaningful trip to Milan. The city's cool elegance and modern pace were balanced by a deep sense of cultural reverence. The highlight was our visit to Leonardo da Vinci's The Last Supper. Hidden within the convent of Santa Maria delle Grazie, the mural revealed itself in a quiet room where centuries of history seemed to pause around us. We also took time to admire the Gothic beauty of the Duomo and strolled beneath the soaring glass ceilings of the Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II. Though it was our first of several trips to Italy, Milan set the tone‚Äîart, history, and timeless beauty, all in one.
