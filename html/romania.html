România

Just a Small Sampling of Our Pin Drops:

  • Bucharest
  • Brașov
  • Transylvania

Driving through Romania's Carpathian Mountains felt like crossing into a Gothic landscape. The winding roads carved through dense forests and misty peaks, creating an atmosphere both serene and mysterious. As we entered Transylvania, the landscape grew even more dramatic—rolling hills dotted with rustic villages, fortified churches, and medieval watchtowers that seemed untouched by time. One of the most memorable stops was Bran Castle, often linked to the Dracula legend. Perched atop a rocky cliff, its towering spires and shadowy stone walls invited us in. We were able to tour both the interior and exterior of the castle, exploring narrow staircases, antique-filled rooms, and panoramic balconies that brought the region's history and lore vividly to life.

We spent time in Brașov, a charming city nestled beneath the Carpathians with colorful baroque buildings, cozy cafés, and a lively central square. Its Black Church and narrow Rope Street offered a unique blend of history and whimsy. Finally, our journey led us to Bucharest, Romania's capital, where centuries-old Orthodox churches sit beside grand communist-era buildings. The city's energy was distinct—part old-world charm, part modern revival—making it a fitting end to a road trip filled with contrasts, legends, and unforgettable views.
