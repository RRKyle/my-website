Ísland

A Few of Our Pin Drops:

  • Reykjavík
  • Þingvellir National Park
  • Strokkur Geyser

We had an incredible time visiting Iceland—it's easily one of the most unique (and coldest) places we've ever been. We started our trip in Reykjavík, hoping to catch a glimpse of the Northern Lights, but unfortunately, the weather didn't cooperate. It was too cloudy and snowy, so the lights stayed hidden—we searched for that elusive Aurora not just in Iceland, but across much of Europe, and no matter where we went, we missed it.

The capital city of Reykjavík had a cool mix of modern style and quirky charm, with colorful buildings and plenty of cozy spots to grab a coffee. You could still feel the roots of its Viking heritage woven into everyday life. From there, we got to see some of Iceland's wild natural beauty. One of the most unforgettable moments was standing between the North American and Eurasian tectonic plates at Þingvellir National Park—pretty surreal to be literally walking between continents. The Icelandic horses, brought to Iceland by Norse settlers ceturies ago, were pretty cool as well

Continuing on outside of the city, the scenery just kept getting better. We saw powerful waterfalls like Gullfoss and Seljalandsfoss, and incredible geysers like Strokkur that shot boiling water straight into the air every few minutes like nature's own fountain. One of the highlights for us was the Game of Thrones tour. Even though some of the filming locations from the show were difficult to fully take in due to the snow, seeing them in real life was a blast, especially since the landscapes already feel straight out of fantasy. Iceland really is like nowhere else—raw, beautiful, and full of surprises.
