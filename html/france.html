La France

Just a Small Sampling of Our Pin Drops:

  • Paris
  • Strasbourg
  • D-Day Normandy Expedition

Our travels through France gave us a rich mix of history, celebration, and old-world charm. The most memorable experience we had was exploring the powerful and historic landscapes of World War II. We toured the D-Day landing beaches, stood atop the rugged cliffs of Pointe du Hoc, and visited the Normandy American Cemetery and Memorial overlooking Omaha Beach—each stop a solemn tribute to the courage and sacrifice of those who fought there. In the town of Sainte-Mère-Église, we saw a striking and enduring symbol of that history: to this day, a mannequin paratrooper hangs from the church steeple, honoring Private John Steele, who was famously caught there during the airborne landings. That same trip also took us to the stunning Mont Saint-Michel, a tidal island crowned by a medieval abbey. Its winding stone streets and panoramic views made it feel like we had stepped into a medieval masterpiece brought to life.

Another unforgettable visit was to Paris for a New Year’s Eve celebration. As the clock approached midnight, the Eiffel Tower lit up in a glittering display, and the air along the Seine buzzed with excitement. Fireworks, cheers, music, and the sounds of many intoxicated individuals filled the night, making it a truly cinematic way to ring in the new year. The following morning was quiet and peaceful. We set out to find a local café and enjoy a good cup of coffee, taking time to reflect on the night before as the city slowly came back to life—then continued on to Notre-Dame Cathedral, still fully intact in all its Gothic splendor before the devastating fire of 2019, where its stained-glass windows and towering façade offered a moment of quiet awe in the heart of the city.

On a separate trip, we visited Strasbourg during the holiday season to experience its world-famous Christmas market. With its timber-framed buildings, festive lights, and cozy aromas of mulled wine and spiced pastries, the city felt like a winter fairytale. Each market stall seemed to glow with festive charm, and the atmosphere felt straight out of a winter fairytale. From historic reflections to joyful celebrations, France gifted us some of the most poignant and lasting memories of our time in Europe.
